Individuals In Recovery

Our Individual's in Active Recovery program is person-driven post-recovery support. The focus is on long-term wellness and stability through growth. Statistics show the first two years of an individual's recovery are the most critical. Our program is positioned to combine various modalities that are customized to support each client's past, present, and future visions.

Our approach is personalized, hands-on, and holistic providing on-site techniques and information that will allow participants to demonstrate and practice new life skills. These skills will prepare and empower them to set and achieve higher standards while correcting current behaviors and unhealthy patterns.

​

Similar to the Corporate Wellness Education program, the Individual's in Active Recovery program takes a holistic and integrative approach to our clients over-all health including all aspects outlined in the Corporate Wellness division.

Support for Individuals in Active Recovery includes customized nutrition based on the individual’s laboratory tests, body movement assessments, health education, customized and targeted nutritional supplementation, Insightful Change sessions & classes, customized stress reduction tools, and personal wellness coaching.

Individuals in Active Recovery - Are individuals who desire continued support to maintain long-term sobriety and stability after completing a foundational recovery program for a minimum of three months.

​

The program for Individuals in Active Recovery includes any combination of the following:
Customized laboratory tests (not all tests will be needed by all clients)

  • Comprehensive Wellness Profile (serum)
  • Food Intolerance / Sensitivity Testing (serum)
  • Adrenal Stress Health Index (saliva)
  • Gut Health Profile (stool)
  • Customized nutrition based on laboratory tests results
  • Customized body movement assessments to reduce stress on mind and body
  • General health education
  • Customized and targeted nutritional supplementation based on lab results
  • Personal sessions and group classes utilizing a variety of self-help stress reduction tools
    • ​
  • Personal and private sessions for trauma recovery
  • Personal wellness coaching
  • Personal and group employment coaching
  • Financial education and planning classes

Our goal is to fine-tune the emotional and physical health of our recovering clients. We use integrative modalities with personally and professionally designed health programs to restore physical and emotional health to peak performance levels. By addressing all levels of health including: nutrition, physical fitness, and emotional & mental health, our process will reduce stress and anxiety. This strengthens resolve to maintain a healthy, happy and sober lifestyle and, as a result, they become better partners, parents, and employees.

The next step will be to place our clients into jobs with our community business partners. We will continue to work with them as they interact with their co-workers and supervisors, addressing any job related stress issues. This step in the process will strengthen self-confidence, reinforce forward movement into their new patterns, and help maintain long term employment.

* Based on information from the National Institute on Drug Abuse, Soberplace, and Foundations Recovery Network, the following statistics give a current picture of recovery:
  • 47% of addicts relapse in 1st year after treatment
  • 61% of those who relapse will do so more than once

* Factors likely to contribute to relapse: mental health state, family history of addiction, the type of substance used, stress level, and a failure to seek quality aftercare

* ​According to Return to Work Knowledge Base, rehabilitation is most successful when it addresses a person's self-efficacy; One's belief in one's ability to succeed in specific situations or accomplish a task through:

  • Previous experience
  • Observations of others' achievements
  • Verbal encouragement
  • Emotional state

* Low self-efficacy can result in a lack of motivation, uncertainty of abilities, fear of failure, missed appointments, and the desire to drop out altogether. Those who lack confidence in their coping ability are often more anxious and distressed. Fear and anxiety cause worry about returning to work. Return to work outcomes can be improved when rehabilitation is aimed at increasing self-efficacy.

* "Forty percent of those who managed only two years of abstinence eventually went back to the bottle. After five years of sobriety, however, relapse was rare." Five Years is Magic Number for Recovering Alcoholics; W.J. Cromie, The Harvard University Gazette, 1996;
